Featured Project

Beverages Ltd — Revenue Risk
& Payment Intelligence

A six-page interactive Power BI report built for a multi-region US beverage distributor. This project spans the full analytics lifecycle — SQL extraction, Excel validation, Power BI modelling, and stakeholder-ready dashboard delivery.

Business Context

Beverages Ltd was selling across three US regions but lacked real-time visibility into payment behaviour. Collections teams operated from fragmented spreadsheets, high-value retailers were breaching 65-day payment cycles, and £40.79K of revenue was silently at risk with no early warning system in place.

The solution: a decision intelligence platform that surfaces risk at the retailer, product, and regional level — before receivables age into bad debt.

Technical Components
Step 01 · SQL Server
Data Extraction & Import
Structured import of transactional sales and payment data from Excel into SQL Server using the Import and Export Wizard, with first-row column header configuration for clean ingestion.
Step 02 · SQL Querying
Data Validation & UNION
Applied UNION operations to consolidate multi-retailer datasets (Costco, Target, Walgreens, Walmart), with WHERE filters and aggregate functions to validate revenue totals and payment cycle metrics.
Step 03 · Power BI
Star Schema Data Model
Built a star schema with BeveragesCo as the central fact table linked to Calendar and a dedicated _Measures table. Many-to-one relationships enable dynamic DAX filtering across all six report pages.
Key Insights Delivered
01

Q1 Policy Enforcement Worked

Average days-to-pay dropped from 34 in January to 30 by April and held steady for 8 consecutive months — a data-confirmed operational win.

02

Costco: Critical Risk

65-day payment cycle (34 days beyond target), 100% red aging profile, and £7,750 outstanding — the portfolio's single largest credit risk driver.

03

Target: Highest Proportional Risk

17.08% of Target's revenue at risk — the highest rate in the portfolio, signalling a structural payment behaviour issue beyond its volume contribution.

04

Brand-Level Risk Cluster

Diet Coke at 65 days and Sprite at 57 days form a dual-brand risk cluster invisible in standard retailer reporting — requiring product-level monitoring.

05

West Region Dominates

West generates £582.40K (47.9% of total revenue), with New York's 360-day collection anomaly flagged as an outlier requiring forensic review.

06

96.64% On-Time Portfolio

Company-wide aging profile shows 96.64% of receivables on time — a strong headline figure that the Accountability Table contextualises with precision.
